TOP DESTINATIONS

MALAPPURAM

Malappuram, a city in the state of Kerala, India, is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty. Nestled amidst rolling hills and lush greenery, Malappuram is famous for its festivals, traditional arts, and historic sites such as the Kottakkunnu hill park and the ancient Thirunavaya temple. The city is a hub of Islamic culture in Kerala, with many mosques and educational institutions. Malappuram also offers scenic landscapes, including rivers, waterfalls, and teak plantations, making it a vibrant and picturesque destination.

KOZHIKODE

Kozhikode, also known as Calicut, is a vibrant city in the state of Kerala, India. Known for its rich history and cultural heritage, Kozhikode was a major trading port during medieval times, attracting traders from Arabia and Europe. The city is famous for its delicious cuisine, including the renowned Malabar biryani and delectable sweets like halwa. Kozhikode's beautiful beaches, such as Kappad Beach where Vasco da Gama landed in 1498, and its bustling markets, like SM Street, make it a captivating destination for visitors.

ABOUT KERALA


Kerala, located in the southwestern region of India, is renowned for its stunning natural beauty, including lush green landscapes, tranquil backwaters, and pristine beaches. Often referred to as "God's Own Country," Kerala boasts a rich cultural heritage, with traditional art forms such as Kathakali dance and Theyyam rituals. The state is also known for its high literacy rate, excellent healthcare system, and diverse cuisine, which features a variety of spices, coconut-based dishes, and seafood. Tourism thrives here, with visitors flocking to enjoy houseboat cruises, wildlife sanctuaries, and Ayurvedic wellness treatments.
